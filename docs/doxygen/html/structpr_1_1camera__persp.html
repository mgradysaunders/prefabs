Emissive importance.

Emissive importance is given by

\[ Z_e(\mathbf{p}, \omega) = \delta(\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{p}_c) \frac{\chi^+(\omega \in \Omega_c)}{A\cos^3\theta} \]

where \( \chi^+(\omega \in \Omega_c) \) is an abuse of notation (of the Heaviside step function) indicating \( \omega \) must be within \( \Omega_c \), the region of solid angle corresponding to the projection of the rectangular screen onto the unit sphere.

By this definition, \( Z_e \) satisfies the normalization condition

\[ \int_{\mathcal{A}} \int_{\mathcal{S}^2} Z_e(\mathbf{p}, \omega) \,d{\omega} \,d\mathcal{A}(\mathbf{p}) = 1. \]

Parameters
[in]wDirection.
[out]rRaster coordinate. Optional.
Note
As the position distribution is a delta, this calculates only the part of \( Z_e \) which depends on \( \omega \).
In Physically based rendering: from theory to implementation (3rd edition), Pharr, Humphreys, and Jakob define emissive importance for perspective cameras as

\[ W_e(\mathbf{p}, \omega) = \delta(\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{p}_c) \frac{\chi^+(\omega \in \Omega_c)}{A\cos^4\theta} \]

which satisfies the alternative normalization condition

\[ \int_{\mathcal{A}} \int_{\mathcal{S}^2} W_e(\mathbf{p}, \omega)|\cos\theta| \,d{\omega} \,d\mathcal{A}(\mathbf{p}) = 1. \]

This is sensible in their implementation, as they allow perspective cameras to have “lenses” with non-zero area. The implementation here, however, requires that perspective cameras are pinholes, i.e., point sources of importance.

Incident importance sampling routine.

Incident importance is given by

\[ Z_i(\omega_i; \mathbf{p}_{\text{ref}}) = \frac{1}{d^2} Z_e(\mathbf{p}_{\text{hit}}, -\omega_i) \]

where \( \mathbf{p}_{\text{hit}} \) is the hit point on the camera (from the ray \( (\mathbf{p}_{\text{ref}}, \omega_i) \)) and \( d \) is the distance between \( \mathbf{p}_{\text{ref}} \) and \( \mathbf{p}_{\text{hit}} \).

This definition uses the convention that a pinhole camera is a point source of importance, giving rise to the \( 1 / d^2 \) falloff. As a direct result, the associated solid angle sampling density is trivially \( f_\omega = \delta(\omega - \omega_i) = \delta(\mathbf{p}_{\text{hit}} - \mathbf{p}_c) \).

Parameters
[in]prefReference point.
[out]wiIncident direction.
[out]rRaster coordinate. Optional.
Returns
Returns incident importance.
Note
In Physically based rendering: from theory to implementation (3rd edition), Pharr, Humphreys, and Jakob define incident importance for perspective cameras as

\[ W_i(\omega_i; \mathbf{p}_{\text{ref}}) = W_e(\mathbf{p}_{\text{hit}}, -\omega_i). \]

This is sensible in their implementation, as they allow perspective cameras to have “lenses” with non-zero area. Thus, they obtain solid angle sampling density from area sampling density as

\[ f_\omega = \frac{d^2}{|\hat{\mathbf{z}}\cdot-\omega_i|} f_{\mathcal{A}} \]

where, for pinhole cameras, \( f_{\mathcal{A}} = \delta(\mathbf{p}_{\text{hit}} - \mathbf{p}_c) \). Moreover, the ratio of \( W_i \) to its sampling density is equivalent to the ratio of \( Z_i \) to its sampling density.
